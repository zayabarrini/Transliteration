Full translation: “Hi, I’m Zaya Barini. I want to share something I learned recently that has surprisingly reshaped my perspective on engineering leadership: Mandarin. Now, I'm hitting a wall with traditional learning methods You know, just repeating characters over and over again or drilling flashcards. It feels chaotic and my brain is trying to find patterns. So, I decided to build a system. I started color coding the characters according to their grammatical category. Suddenly, a wall of black and white symbols transformed into a structured visual map. I created my own "grammar" of understanding, and instantly, things that were overwhelming became manageable, even enjoyable. This is where it relates directly to my engineering management philosophy. As leaders, our role is to be the system architects for our teams. We continue to face complex and ambiguous challenges A massive product roadmap, legacy systems that need tidying up, or a new technology strategy. Our job is not to have all the answers, but to create the framework and clarity that allows our teams to navigate this complexity without being overwhelmed. We build visualizations, establish clear processes, and develop a communication rhythm to transform chaos into focused, effective work. I even found myself applying it to playlists for EXO's Chinese albums, color-coding the songs by theme and energy. It turns the passive listening activity into an active and systematic learning tool. It proved to me that this principle of "clear structure" is universally applicable. So, the most important lesson for me is: Whether you're learning a language, organizing a playlist, or leading an engineering team, the goal is the same. Great leadership is about designing systems and environments that make complex things simple. This is about reducing cognitive load so that talented people can do their most creative and impactful work. This is the mentality One of the systems thinkers and clarity creators I'm excited to take on the role of Engineering Manager at Buffer. "
