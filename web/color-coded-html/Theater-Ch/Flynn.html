Full translation: What do you do when you're unsure? This is the theme of my sermon today. Who among us did not experience the most profound loss last year, when President Kennedy was assassinated? despair? Which way? What now? What should I say to my child? What do I tell myself? It was a time when people sat together, united by a shared sense of despair. But think about this. Your connection to your companions is your despair. This is a communal experience. It sucks, but we're in this together. How bad is it for a lonely man, a lonely woman, who suffers a private disaster? "No one knew I was sick." "No one knew I had lost my last true friend." "No one knows what I did wrong." Imagine that feeling of isolation. Now you see the world through the window. On one side of the glass are happy and carefree people, on the other side is you. There are some in the church today who know exactly the crisis of faith I am describing, and I want to say to you: Doubt can be as powerful and a lasting bond as certainty. When you are lost, you are not alone.
